Gratified — Business & Operations Plan (5 Pages)

1. Executive Summary

Gratified proposes to operate a community-focused café at the Lower Level of San Francisco’s Main Library. The café will emphasize nourishing drinks made with high-quality ingredients, collaborative partnerships with local producers, and a creative environment that supports art, meetups, and the needs of a technologically-embedded generation seeking in-person connection.

The proposed rental structure is 8% of gross sales with a \$250 minimum monthly guarantee. Our launch strategy keeps operations simple (drinks-only first 2–3 months), ensuring compliance with no-cook constraints and maintaining lean overhead.

Gratified currently has \$50,000 available to start and sustain the Library location (\$40,000 personal capital + \$10,000 equity). A larger \$250,000 raise is planned for brand consolidation later in 2025, but is not required for the Library pilot.

Owner/operator Leah McCuan brings 5 years of experience in operations, management, communications, and technical coordination — including roles supporting executive leadership at Anthropic, Palisade Research, and Theorem Labs.

2. Concept & Mission

Gratified aims to create a space that is:

  • Nourishing: clean, low-sweet drinks; low-carb bites; high-quality ingredients.
  • Collaborative: partnerships with local roasters, bakers, and artists.
  • Creative: work-in-progress aesthetic, art displays, meetup support.

The Main Library is a multi-floor hub of research, events, and civic culture. The lower-level café space provides an ideal environment for Gratified to serve regular patrons, event-goers, students, and staff while adding an energizing and community-building element to the Library ecosystem.

3. Startup Costs

Estimated startup costs for the Library location (first 1–3 months):

  • \$5,000 — permitting & compliance
  • \$5,000 — initial inventory
  • \$12,000 — espresso machine, grinder, small appliances (microwaves, blender)
  • \$8,000 — marketing, legal, graphic/brand consolidation
  • \$10,000 — operations buffer for stocking, repairs, payroll

Total: approx. \$40,000–\$50,000

4. Funding Plan

  • \$10,000 — existing equity investment
  • \$250,000 — planned March raise (not required for Library opening)

6. Operations & Staffing

Core operations will follow Library hours. Proposed service:

  • Mon 9am–6pm
  • Tues–Thu 9am–8pm
  • Fri 12pm–6pm
  • Sat 10am–6pm
  • Sun 12pm–6pm

Staffing Plan

  • Month 1–3: Owner full-time + 1 part-time barista
  • Month 3+: Add second PT barista as demand increases
  • Off-site partners for pastries and packaged food

Equipment & Layout

  • Espresso machine + grinder
  • Pour-over station
  • Microwave(s), blender, kettles (all plug-in; compliant with no-cook restrictions)
  • Charging stations integrated into bench seating if permitted
  • Optional water station & refill setup

A simple black-and-white layout diagram will be submitted as RFP Appendix C.

7. Menu & Pricing

Drinks

  • \$5 Simple: drip, espresso, cappuccino, latte, chai
  • \$10 Special: single-origin pour-over, ceremonial matcha, premium hot chocolate
  • \$15 Audacious: seasonal drinks (fresh fruit purées, protein add-ons, longevity blends)

Subscription

\$108/month — unlimited simple & special drinks (up to 3/day). Proven successful at test locations for building community and steady revenue.

Food

Launch drinks-only for first 2–3 months. Add small off-site–prepared items later (egg tarts, whole-grain items, low-sweet snacks).

8. Sustainability Commitments

  • Compostable to-go serviceware
  • Three-bin waste system with clear signage
  • 80% of coffee/food sourced locally
  • No artificial colors/flavors; whole-ingredient focus
  • Long-term goal: support more efficient trade routes with East African coffee producers
  • Employee handbook (Month 3) with sustainability training & customer-education notes

9. Financial Summary

Projected Monthly Revenue (Month 3)

  • Simple drinks: 160/day × \$5 × 30 = \$24,000
  • Special drinks: 30/day × \$10 × 30 = \$9,000
  • Audacious: 10/day × \$15 × 30 = \$4,500
  • Subscriptions: 45 × \$108 = \$4,860

Estimated total ≈ \$42,000/month

Rent

8% of gross sales = approx. \$3,360/month at projected Month-3 revenue. Meets City expectations for percentage-based café permits.

Monthly Operating Costs

  • Payroll: \$7,000–\$9,000
  • Inventory: \$8,000–\$10,000
  • Rent (8%): \$3,000–\$3,500
  • Utilities/insurance/misc: \$2,000–\$2,800

Projected operating margin Month-3: 18–25%.
